Bins with less than nmin samples were over-sampled by replicating all samples as often as they fit into nmin. To fill up, additional samples were randomly drawn until nmin was reached. Thus, the area bin > 90000 m2 is replicated 3 times The ratio between the most abundant bin and the least abundant bin is therefore 2:1 in the training dataset. As there is no recommended value for this ratio, we chose a value that keeps a reasonable amount of samples in the high abundance bins without too strongly over-sampling the low abundance bins. Data was unaffected, as it was separated prior to balancing the dataset. Using the validation data, we compared predictions based on training with and without considering balancing. The balancing decreased the overall performance in terms of the MAE, however, the models generalized better across the iceberg size range.
