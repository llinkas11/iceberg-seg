| `figS01_otsu_floor_distribution` | [20260505_150805__q07_otsu_floor_distribution__overview.png](../figure_review/script_check_answers/q07_otsu_floor_distribution/20260505_150805__q07_otsu_floor_distribution__overview.png) | n/a | Per-chip Otsu threshold on Sentinel-2 B08 reflectance over all 23,981 study chips. Red dashed line: production noise-floor of 0.10 (offset-uncorrected reflectance) used in `otsu_threshold_tifs.py`. Grey dashed lines: alternative candidates 0.15 and 0.20. Dotted blue lines: offset-corrected positions (+0.10) of each candidate. Skip-rates over the full chip set: 0.10 removes 6.08 % of chips (1,458 / 23,981), 0.15 removes 41.02 % (9,838 / 23,981), and 0.20, the offset-corrected equivalent of 0.10, removes 50.54 % (12,120 / 23,981). The narrow spike just above 0.10 is the Sentinel-2 L1C baseline 04.00 +0.10 radiometric offset: ocean-only chips with no real iceberg signal return Otsu thresholds at the offset noise floor. The 0.10 cutoff removes those noise-floor chips while preserving the 0.10 to 0.30 distribution mass that carries real iceberg / water boundaries; raising the floor to 0.20 would discard ~45 % of chips with legitimate boundaries. This result fixes the OT noise-floor at 0.10 in offset-uncorrected reflectance and rules out the 0.20 (offset-corrected) interpretation; the production setting in `otsu_threshold_tifs.py` follows from this finding. Source script: `iceberg-rework/scripts/script_check_answers/q07_otsu_floor_distribution.py`. |
